# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

High school and college students (ages 15–22) managing 5–7 classes, extracurriculars, practice schedules, and a personal life simultaneously. They open their planner during moments of stress — usually the night before something is due. They're not lazy; they're under-tooled. Their context is mobile-first but they discover the brand via desktop/social. They feel overwhelmed by deadlines scattered across different apps with no sense of *when* to actually do the work.

## Product Purpose

Prio converts natural language task input ("English essay due Friday, about 2 hours") into a smart daily plan with time blocked for every assignment, ordered by due date, difficulty, and available time. It removes the gap between "I know what's due" and "I know when to do it." Free forever on iOS and Android. Pre-launch, so the site is a holding page + brand story.

## Brand Personality

Calm. Personal. Honest.

The emotional goal: the student should feel like a friend who really gets it built this for them — not a startup trying to sell them something. The site should feel more spacious than a student's calendar (less stressed, not more), like opening a clean notebook vs. a cluttered backpack.

## Anti-references

- Generic dark-glass SaaS (Linear/Vercel clones: near-black + neon glow + glassmorphism badges)
- Over-animated portfolio sites (scroll-jacking, stacked parallax, every element flying in)
- Loud edtech/freemium apps (Quizlet, Kahoot: primary-color soup, excessive iconography)
- "AI product" template: dark bg, glowing gradient orbs, HUD counters, particle canvas

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The site itself should feel calm. If the brand's promise is "less stress," the page cannot be busier than a normal calendar.
2. **Show the product, not the concept.** Use real UI — mini-calendars, the daily plan, the type-to-plan demo — as the primary visuals, not abstract illustrations.
3. **One student, first person.** The mission story and voice are personal ("I had the calendar. I still crammed."), not corporate ("Prio helps students…").
4. **White space is a feature.** Density is the enemy of calm. Resist the urge to fill every section.
5. **Earned motion only.** Every animation must serve comprehension or confirm an action. No ambient loops.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. prefers-reduced-motion collapses all animations to instant transitions. Color is never the sole differentiator. Font size floor: 13px. Contrast target: 4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text.
