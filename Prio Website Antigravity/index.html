Calendars just show deadlines

They tell you when things are due — not when to work on them. That gap is where procrastination lives.

Cramming isn't a you problem

Students don't procrastinate because they're lazy. They do it because no tool actually helps them plan their work.

Prio fills that gap

Type one sentence. Prio reads it, estimates your workload, assigns priority, and builds your entire day automatically.

Everything you need. Nothing you don't.

Prio is built by students who were tired of juggling six classes, practice, and a social life with tools that weren't built for them.

Just type it.

'English essay due Friday, probably 2 hours' — that's all it takes. Prio reads plain English and builds the plan from there. No forms, no dropdowns.

Your day, planned for you.

Each morning, Prio builds a prioritized checklist of exactly what to work on — balanced around your free time and sorted by urgency automatically.

A timer that actually helps.

Start a session and Prio counts down through your tasks. Finish one, it moves to the next. Running out of time? Low-priority tasks shift to tomorrow on their own.

Big projects, small steps.

Research papers and long-term assignments get broken into AI-generated subtasks with their own deadlines. A progress bar shows exactly how far you've come.
